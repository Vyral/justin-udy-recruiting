Anything you want to do will be 10x harder than you thought it would be to accomplish.

Schedule a 30 Min Sit Down Today

Reaching the next level in your career and achieving all your goals means following one simple guiding principle: Honor the struggle.

I learned about this principle while attending a three-day workshop run by a man named Brendon Berchard, and once he said it to me, it rang so true that I started mentioning it to my own team.

What does it mean to honor the struggle? Let me give you an example.

For my team and I to get from 200 transactions to 350, we’ll face the same struggle I faced to get from 100 to 200, and before that, getting from 50 to 100. In other words, going from one benchmark to the next takes double the amount of work and activity.

Anything you want to do will be 10x harder than you thought it would be to accomplish. I don’t say that in a negative way—I say it to get you motivated and excited! You know as well as I do that you’ll have 10x the blockades you previously had to reach your next goal, but once you do, you’ll get 100x more success. This is because you’re doing more, committing more, and accepting more of what you know will come your way and breaking through it, whereas other people—as much as I hate to say it—will shirk that same challenge, stop progressing, and do only enough to support their life instead of creating a bigger life for themselves.

“
Anything you want to do will be 10x harder than you thought it would be to accomplish.

”

So, honor the struggle, honor the work, and know that it’s going to take time, effort, and energy to get to the next level. If you’re averaging one deal a month, do two deals from now on. If you’re average two, I want you to shoot for three. I know you can do it.

When I was on my own, I did 48 transactions one year with just a part-time assistant, so I know what it takes. Then the year I did 85 listings on my own, it was a completely different experience growing to that point.

One tool I recommend you use to achieve the results you want is a daily activity record. I used one to keep myself accountable and make sure my business grew so that I grew too and I could make a bigger life for myself and the people I work with.
